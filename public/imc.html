Immigration Medical Clearance (IMC) – Application Process

The Immigration Medical Clearance (IMC) is a mandatory health screening requirement for selected categories of employment in Bahrain, particularly in sectors where physical performance and medical fitness are essential, such as professional sports. This process ensures that all incoming personnel meet the required medical standards prior to finalizing visa sponsorship and entry clearance procedures.

Who Requires IMC: The IMC is required for candidates where the employer specifies pre-arrival medical clearance as part of the recruitment and sponsorship process. This is commonly applicable to professional athletes and other roles involving high physical demand, where medical fitness is a key requirement for participation. You should only proceed with this process once your prospective employer has confirmed that IMC is required for your specific case.

How the Process Works:

  1. Employer Confirmation: Your prospective employer confirms the requirement for Immigration Medical Clearance as part of your onboarding and sponsorship process.
  2. Medical Coordination Code Issuance: An official IMC coordination code is generated to link your medical file to your entry permit/sponsorship application.
  3. Medical Examination Arrangement: The medical examination is completed either:
    • In your home country at a licensed and approved medical facility, or
    • At a designated medical facility in the country of departure or transit, depending on your confirmed travel itinerary and airline routing to Bahrain.
  4. Submission of Medical Results: The completed medical report is submitted through the approved channel for review and validation under Bahrain’s immigration health requirements.
  5. Visa Sponsorship Finalization: Once medical clearance is approved, the employer proceeds with the finalization of the sponsorship and employment visa application process.

Important Notice:

  • Do not proceed with any medical examination unless officially instructed by your employer.
  • Only medical reports completed through approved facilities and in the correct format will be accepted.
  • Failure to follow the correct procedure may result in invalid documentation and delays in processing.

Start Your IMC Application

The process is initiated through an official Letter of Intent submitted to the Immigration Health Coordination Office.

Send Letter of Intent

Once received, an Immigration Health Officer will issue your IMC coordination code and provide further instructions for the next steps.

Note for Desktop Users: If the email does not open automatically when clicking the button, please ensure that you have a default email application set (such as Outlook, Apple Mail, Thunderbird, or Gmail in your browser).

Alternatively, you can send your Letter of Intent directly to: booking@npra.gov.bh

IMC Processing Fee: Approx. 250 BHD (subject to change based on the medical provider or location)
